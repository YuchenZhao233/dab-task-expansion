HINTS:
- Account identifiers do not share a single format across the two databases: in
  context_db the account key carries a non-numeric prefix, while in facts_db the
  same accounts are referenced by their bare numeric id. Reconcile the formats
  before matching accounts across databases.
- Transaction type codes are Czech: PRIJEM means a credit (money in), VYDAJ and
  VYBER mean withdrawals (money out).
- Transaction and order purpose codes (k_symbol) are Czech: SIPO denotes a
  household payment, POJISTNE denotes an insurance payment, UVER a loan payment,
  DUCHOD a pension, UROK interest, SLUZBY a statement charge.
- Statement-issuance frequency codes are Czech: POPLATEK MESICNE means monthly,
  POPLATEK TYDNE means weekly, POPLATEK PO OBRATU means after each transaction.
- Loan status is a single letter: A means the contract finished with no problems,
  B means the contract finished but the loan was not paid, C means a running
  contract so far in order, D means a running contract that is in debt. A loan is
  considered defaulted when it was not paid or is in debt.
- A client's role on an account is given by the disposition type: OWNER is the
  account holder; a single account may also have DISPONENT users.
- District demographic attributes use coded column names; the region is the
  region-name attribute, the number of inhabitants is the inhabitants attribute,
  and the average salary is the average-salary attribute. A region spans several
  districts, so a region-level inhabitant count is the sum of its districts'
  inhabitant values; treat any inhabitant value as a number even if stored as text.
- An account's region or district is the district recorded on the account itself,
  which is not necessarily the home district recorded on the account's clients.
- A card's issuance date is the date in its issued field; when an account has more
  than one card of the same tier, the earliest such issuance date applies.
- A client's age at a given date is the elapsed time from their birth date to
  that date; compute it in whole years (for example, using a 365.25-day year).
- A per-1000-inhabitants metric divides the summed amount by the relevant
  inhabitant count and multiplies by 1000.
