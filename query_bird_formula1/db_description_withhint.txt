HINTS:
- Lap times, qualifying session times (q1/q2/q3) and pit-stop durations are stored only as formatted strings. They may appear as plain seconds ("23.227"), as minutes-and-seconds with one colon ("1:21.046"), or, for lap times only, as hours-minutes-seconds with two colons ("2:05:07.547") when a lap spans a red-flag suspension. Convert every value to total seconds before comparing, ranking or averaging, and account for all of these forms. An occasional qualifying entry is corrupted (two colons); treat it as missing and use the driver's other sessions.
- A driver's qualifying time for a session is empty when no time was set in it; a driver's best qualifying time for a race is the smallest of their non-empty session times.
- Finishing status is stored as a code that must be resolved against the status descriptions. Classifying an outcome requires interpreting the status text: an on-track crash can be described in several different ways, and it must be distinguished from mechanical failures, lapped finishes and other non-crash retirements.
- A driver counts as a classified finisher in a numeric position only when the textual finishing position is an actual number; non-numeric codes denote retirements or other non-finishes.
- Modern Formula 1 awards championship points 25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1 to the first through tenth classified finishers of a race and nothing below tenth. Points actually stored reflect whatever system was in effect that season. Some historical seasons also applied a "best results count" rule, under which only a fixed number of a driver's strongest race scores contribute to the championship total and the rest are dropped.
- The pitStops table has no team column; a pit stop's constructor is the team the driver drove for in that race, available from that driver's result row for the same race.
- Ages and elapsed times should be computed from the relevant dates; date of birth and race dates are given as YYYY-MM-DD.
- Standings tables hold a cumulative snapshot taken after each race, so a championship position can be occupied after many different races; the standing after the final round of a season is the one taken after the season's highest-numbered round.
- When a question asks for a "typical" or per-season figure across multiple seasons that contain different numbers of races, average within each season first and then across seasons, so that busier seasons do not dominate.
- When a ranking could be decided by a few extreme or non-representative records (outlier durations, suspension-affected laps, tiny samples), apply the stated exclusion and minimum-count rules before ranking.
